Mental Wellness and Empowering Grassroot Resilience of IDPs & PWDs

A green resilience project supporting displaced communities with psychosocial care, dignity restoration, and pathways into practical circular livelihoods.

Through this initiative, JullyConsult supports Internally Displaced Persons, women, girls, youth, and Persons with Disabilities in flood-affected and displaced communities. The project combines mental wellness education, safe healing spaces, and green vocational skills to help participants rebuild confidence, restore dignity, and create sustainable sources of income.

Restoring Hope, Dignity, and Livelihoods in Displaced Communities

The Mental Wellness and Empowering Grassroot Resilience of IDPs & PWDs project was created to respond to the emotional, social, and economic struggles faced by displaced people, especially those affected by flooding, conflict, poverty, and environmental degradation.

Many displaced women, girls, youth, and Persons with Disabilities face trauma, loss of livelihood, social exclusion, and limited access to support systems. JullyConsult addresses these challenges by combining trauma-informed psychosocial support with hands-on green skills training.

Participants are supported to heal, learn, and rebuild their lives through safe learning spaces, mental wellness sessions, and practical training in circular economy skills such as transforming textile and plastic waste into useful products.

Our Project Focus

This initiative integrates mental wellness support with community empowerment for Internally Displaced Persons. It connects trauma-informed care with practical green skills so participants can rebuild confidence, learn, and participate in local circular solutions.

1

Psychosocial Support and Safe Learning Spaces

We create safe, inclusive, and supportive spaces where displaced women, girls, youth, and PWDs can openly learn about mental wellness, emotional resilience, stress management, self-confidence, and community healing.

2

Grassroot Resilience for Flood-Affected and Displaced People

The project strengthens the resilience of communities affected by flooding, displacement, and poverty by helping participants develop coping strategies, practical skills, and community support networks.

3

Green Skills Pathways Linked to Dignity and Income Generation

Participants are trained to collect and transform textile and plastic waste into sustainable products such as reusable pads, bags, berets, artworks, fashion accessories, and other eco-friendly items.

Why This Work Matters

Displacement does not only take away shelter; it also affects dignity, mental health, confidence, income, and hope. Many IDPs, especially women, girls, and Persons with Disabilities, experience emotional stress while also struggling to meet daily needs.

At the same time, flood-affected communities often face environmental pollution caused by plastic and textile waste blocking waterways and worsening flooding. This project responds to both human and environmental challenges by using waste as a tool for healing, creativity, and livelihood restoration.

By combining mental wellness and green vocational skills, JullyConsult helps participants move from vulnerability to resilience, from trauma to confidence, and from waste to sustainable economic opportunity.

Through this project, JullyConsult has supported displaced and underserved communities by equipping participants with the tools to heal, learn, earn, and lead change in their environment.

What We Do

Mental Wellness Education

We provide basic mental health awareness, emotional support, confidence-building sessions, and safe conversations to help participants manage stress, trauma, and anxiety.

Green Vocational Skills Training

We train participants to turn waste materials such as discarded cloth, plastic bottles, and bottle caps into useful and marketable products.

Dignity and Confidence Restoration

We support participants to see themselves as capable creators, entrepreneurs, and climate solution leaders.

Practical Product Development

Participants learn how to design and produce reusable pads, eco-bags, berets, artworks, fashion items, and other circular products.

Access to Enterprise Support

Outstanding participants are supported with microgrants, while others are connected to microfinance institutions for zero-interest loans to establish their green enterprises. All participants are connected to local markets and receive mentorship and follow-up support.

Community Climate Action

The project reduces waste pollution, supports recycling, and promotes local climate resilience in flood-affected communities.

From Displacement to Resilience

Many participants joined the project with limited confidence, emotional stress, and no clear source of income. Through the training, they received psychosocial support, learned new skills, and discovered that they could create useful products from materials often seen as waste.

For many women and girls, the project became more than a training. It became a safe space to heal, build friendships, regain self-worth, and begin a journey toward financial independence.

"Before this training, I did not know that waste materials could become something valuable. I also learned how to manage stress and believe in myself again. Now I can make products and support myself."

Project participant
