ESG COMMITMENT

ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) reflects how JullyConsult impacts the environment, society, and the way we operate responsibly. These three pillars guide our decisions, ensuring that our activities support ecological sustainability, social inclusion, and transparent governance.

At JullyConsult, ESG is at the core of our approach to circular textile waste management, water conservation, climate action, and value creation.

OUR PROMISES, STRATEGY AND TARGETS

Environment

We are committed to advancing circular textile management, ensuring that every garment is given a second life, either through reuse, redesign, or recycling. By operating at the highest level of the waste hierarchy, we significantly reduce environmental impact and conserve water. This is evidenced by the carbon emissions saved and the water that would otherwise be used to produce new textiles.

We set measurable environmental and social targets and report our progress transparently. Within our operational scope, we aim to achieve:

  • 2% reduction in CO2 emissions (Scope 1, 2, and transportation)
  • 4% increase in textile waste collection
  • 5% increase in textile sorting capacity
  • 6% increase in water conservation
  • 7% increase in cultural heritage preservation through sustainable fashion and artworks

We continuously monitor the proportion of textile waste sent to landfills and strive to keep it as close to 0% as possible in the communities we serve.



Circular Partnerships & Collection Impact

We actively collaborate with tailoring shops, textile manufacturers, second-hand markets, municipalities, and retailers to strengthen circular textile systems. Through the deployment of specialized waste carts and collection bins in strategic locations, we have: Collected over 4,610 tonnes of post-consumer textile waste. Recovered more than 5,100 tonnes of post-industrial textile waste from tailoring shops and manufacturers. These partnerships enable us to scale sustainable solutions while reducing environmental pollution and supporting green livelihoods.

SOCIAL RESPONSIBILITY

We are committed to demonstrating social responsibility both internally and externally, in alignment with our circular economy model. Internally, we uphold strong policies that promote employee wellbeing, while externally, we actively engage with communities to drive sustainable and inclusive impact.

We also promote diversity by crafting and designing cultural attires and symbols. We do not discriminate against any culture when creating a customer's outfit, whether Ijaw, Igbo, Yoruba, Hausa, Efik, Kalabari, Indian, Ugandan, Ghanaian, Tunisian, or others. We value all cultures and display them in our gallery shops and exhibitions, where each outfit reflects personalities, occasions, and cultural heritage.

We take the responsibility to recognize and value the diversity of our people. That is why we offer a favorable working environment and conditions allowing our employees to balance the performance of their duties and personal commitments in respect of their own capabilities and desire for development. Our DEI policy and Equal Opportunities Plan offer interventions in five key areas where strategic and practical approaches are needed to empower different social groups. These areas are:

  • Management engagement
  • Inclusive recruitment
  • Equitable remuneration
  • Inclusive culture in the workplace
  • Representativeness in management
Community Engagement & Environmental Awareness

We actively promote responsible consumption and environmental awareness within local communities. Through targeted initiatives, we engage children, youth, and adults in learning about textile reuse, proper disposal, and circular fashion practices.

Our activities include:

  • Educational programs and awareness campaigns
  • School outreach and training sessions
  • Hands-on workshops in redesign, recreation, and repurposing of clothing
  • Capacity-building initiatives that extend the lifespan of garments

We also welcome visits to our facilities from students, business leaders, and stakeholders, offering them first-hand insight into our operations and impact.

To further demonstrate the environmental benefits of responsible textile disposal, we developed the Textile Eco-Green Calculator, a tool that quantifies how simple actions, such as properly discarding a T-shirt, contribute positively to the environment.

CORPORATE GOVERNANCE & ETHICS

JullyConsult operates with a strong commitment to transparency, accountability, and ethical business practices. We align our operations with recognized international standards and best practices, including:

  • Global Recycling Standard (GRS)
  • Eco-Management and Audit Scheme (EMAS)
  • Quality Management Systems
  • Environmental Management Systems
  • Occupational Health and Safety Management Systems

Our Code of Conduct and Anti-Bribery Policy guide our operations across the entire value chain, ensuring fair competition, integrity, and ethical behavior in all our production and supply chain activities.

Partnerships & Industry Collaboration

As part of our sustainability commitment, we actively participate in projects that focus on:

  • Advancing innovative circular textile solutions.
  • Enhancing water conservation and preservation through our model and introduction of community “Water Preservation Ambassadors”.
  • Educating communities and stakeholders on sustainable practices
  • Reducing environmental challenges such as flooding and climate change

We are also proud members of several local, national, and international sustainability and textile recycling organizations. Through these collaborations, we work alongside industry partners to scale effective circular solutions and strengthen the global movement toward sustainable fashion.
