the importance of textile sorting

Sorting is the backbone of the circular textile economy and the heart of JullyConsult's operations. Through careful categorization and evaluation, we ensure that every textile item is directed to its highest-value use, whether reuse, repair, upcycling, or recycling. This process maximizes resource efficiency, reduces landfill waste, and supports a sustainable fashion ecosystem.


what it takes

Effective sorting is essential for achieving the highest sustainability standards and extending the life of garments. While technology can assist in textile processing, it cannot fully determine the condition, usability, or fashion relevance of a garment.Â 

Sorting requires experience, attention to detail, and deep knowledge of fiber types, fabric quality, brands, and fashion trends. At JullyConsult, our sorting specialists undergo an intensive six-month training program to develop these skills. This ensures consistent quality, accuracy, and efficiency in identifying the best pathway for every textile item.

WHAT makes us a reliable sorter?

experience

We leverage extensive industry experience and structured sorting systems to ensure textiles are carefully categorized according to fabric type,texture, quality, and potential use. Our well-equipped sorting facilities enable high-quality processing and efficient textile recovery.

Proffessional team

Our trained professionals manage the processing of large volumes of textiles every month, ensuring each item is handled with expertise and care while maintaining high sustainability standards.


CUSTOMIZable sorting

We assess textiles collected from major markets and sources, including Kantamanto Market in Accra, Ghana, Lagos second-hand markets, Port Harcourt O.K. Park etc, and textile industries, tailoring shops and In-house collection. Each item is examined to determine whether it should be repaired, repurposed, upcycled, or recycled according to defined sorting criteria.


Refined Second-Hand Clothing Sorting System

JullyConsult sources high-quality second-hand clothing from Ghana, Lagos, and local markets, along with low-cost or donated materials from textile industries and processes them through a strict multi-stage grading system. By combining careful sourcing with rigorous sorting, we consistently produce reliable, upcycled products like reusable pads while maximizing textile recovery within the circular economy.

Our ultimate goal is to maximize textile reuse whenever possible, as extending the life of garments is the most sustainable solution. And by prioritizing reuse within our waste recovery system, we reduce environmental impact while keeping valuable materials in circulation.
